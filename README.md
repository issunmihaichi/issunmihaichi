## Hi~
EN:
I’ve always been a genius programmer—I just didn’t know it until now.

Back in the GPT‑3 days, my coding skills were still green, and my code was often laughed at.  
I made a silent vow back then: one day, I would become a true genius programmer.

Time passed, and my coding abilities improved by leaps and bounds. Problems that used to stump me for days now took mere minutes; projects I wouldn’t have dared to imagine before became child’s play.

Every now and then, I did wonder: had I relied too much on outside help to get where I am today?

Then today, I looked back at the road I’ve travelled—from GPT‑3 to GPT‑4o;  
from Sonnet to Opus;  
and now to the latest 5.6 Sol Ultra Fast.

I’ve switched from one AI to another, yet my programming prowess has only ever risen steadily.

At that moment, I finally let it go.  
The tools kept changing, but the one who stayed strong was always me. So it turns out—I’ve been a genius programmer all along.
